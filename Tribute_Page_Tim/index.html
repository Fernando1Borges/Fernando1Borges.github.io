A Brief Timeline of Tim Berner-Lee's Life

  • 1955 Born on June 8th in London, England

  • 1973 Begins studying physics at The Queen's College, Oxford

  • 1976 Graduates with a first-class degree bachelor of arts in physics and begins working as an engineer at a telecomunications company.

  • 1980 Works as an idependent contractor at CERN, proposes a project that uses hypertext to share and update information among researchers. Builds a prototype called ENQUIRE.

  • 1984 Returns to CERN as a fellow after working for several years at a computer company that gave him experience in computer networking.

  • 1989 Berners-Lee write's a proposal combining several existing technologies to create the World Wide Web.

  • 1991 The first website is built and goes online on August 6th at CERN.

  • 1994 Founds the W3C which comprises of companies that are willing to create standards and make recommendations to improve the quality of the web. Decides to make the technology royalty free so that it could be easily adapted by anyone.

  • 2004 Accepts a chair in computer science at the School of Electronics and Computer Science, University of Southampton, Hampshire, to work on the Semantic Web.

  • 2009 Berners-Lee launched the World Wide Web Foundation in order to "advance the Web to empower humanity by launching transformative programs that build local capacity to leverage the Web as a medium for positive change."

  • 2012 Berners-Lee is president of the Open Data Institute.

  • 2013 The Alliance for Affordable Internet (A4AI) was launched in October and Berners-Lee is leading the coalition of public and private organisations that includes Google, Facebook, Intel, and Microsoft. The A4AI seeks to make Internet access more affordable so that access is broadened in the developing world, where only 31% of people are online.
